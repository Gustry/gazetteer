About Glossary

Search by name

Enter a name or search term eg. ’pond‘ in the search box above the map, and press ‘Enter’ or click the icon. Results will be shown in the Matches Found panel, to the right of the map.

Note: When you zoom into the map, only matching names, or search terms, located within the area displayed in the map will be shown. Zoom out to increase the search area.

Deleting the name, or search term, in the search box will clear the search.

The total number of names that match your search criteria is displayed in the panel heading. The names are listed alphabetically and this list is limited to a preset number of results. To reduce the number of names found, you may need to refine your search. 

You can refine your search in two ways:

  1. refining the search term and/or
  2. zooming in.

Selecting an area of the map and zooming in

You can zoom into an area on the map by:

  • clicking on the icon, and drawing a rectangle over the area you’re interested in. 
    - Note: Remember to select the icon afterwards to return the cursor to pointer mode (allowing panning around the map - see Panning below)
  • drawing a rectangle by holding down the shift key and dragging the mouse pointer.
  • using the scale bar and directional arrows at the top left of the map.
  • double clicking on the map.

Panning around the map

The icon allows you to pan around the map by clicking and dragging on the map.  As the map view changes, the names on the Matches Found panel will update automatically. Note that if you are at maximum extent (viewing all of New Zealand) you will not be able to pan around the map.

Searching by location

You can find all features within an area by zooming in to the higher zoom levels.

With the search box clear, zoom into the relevant area until the Matches Found returns the features in that area. As you pan or zoom the map further the Matches Found panel will refresh automatically. You can further refine your search as described above.

Resetting your search

You can quickly reset your search using the following:

  • Changing or clearing the text in the search box: This resets the results in the Matches Found panel, and retains the current zoom level.
  • Selecting the map control icon : This resets the zoom and retains the text in the search box. 
  • Pressing Ctrl F5: This resets the Gazetteer webpage.

Identifying place names on the map

To see the name corresponding to a feature on the map, ensure that the icon is selected and place your mouse cursor over either the feature on the map or the place name listed in the Matches Found panel.  The feature will change from orange to yellow and the place name will also appear on the map at the bottom left.  

Click either the feature on the map or the name listed in the Matches Found panel for more details. See below for description of Details Panel.

Tips

The icon allows you to return to your previous map positions. Click the icon to move forward.  

Along the right side of the map view are two symbols.  Clicking on the top symbol allows you to turn the overlays (e.g. name labels) on and off.  The Basemap cannot be turned off.

Clicking on the bottom symbol opens an overview map that shows the area you are currently viewing in the main map. Moving the red target box will pan the main map view.

Clicking on the panel headings How to Use, Matches Found and Details, will open the selected panel.

The Base Map

The map provides some topographic layer names at certain scales to provide context and help with navigating the map.

Matches found

This panel provides a list of names that meet your search criteria.

If a name is official, it will be shown in bold.

If a feature is located beyond the maximum extent of the map, a corresponding message will be displayed beneath the name.

When you click on a name listed in the Matches Found panel or on a highlighted feature on the map, the Details panel will appear, providing more detail on that particular name or feature.

Details

The Details panel shows information about the name selected, as well as information about the feature itself and any other names for that feature.

To return to the list of names, click on the Matches Found panel heading.

Glossary

Official Name: The name of a geographic feature assigned, approved, altered or validated by the New Zealand Geographic Board, including names under other statutory references (such as Treaty settlements).

Geographic Feature: A physical or cultural object to which a name can be given. The jurisdiction of the New Zealand Geographic Board covers:

  • a natural feature such as a mountain, peak, valley, glen, forest, lagoon, swamp, creek, stream, river, ford, lake, glacier or ice feature, bay, island or harbour (including man-made features of the same type)
  • a railway or railway station, but not a railway feature such as marshalling yards, transfer sites or track point locations
  • a place
  • an undersea feature.

Place: A city, town, village, site, area, locality, suburb or similar place and includes a locality and suburb. It excludes Crown protected areas, and districts, regions and wards of local authorities.

Statutory Reference: The Act of Parliament under which:

  • A Treaty name became official
  • The Deed of Settlement listing a Treaty name is enacted
  • A Crown protected area is held
  • An official name is discontinued (if this isn’t as a result of a determination by the NZGB)
